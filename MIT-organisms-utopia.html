Organisms Utopia

Have you ever thought about the underwater world of the future?

Yuhui Qi / Ruby Wang
Speculative Design / Concept Design
2024
/ 01 Overview

In this new era where the oceans dominate 95% of the planet, humanity has undergone an evolutionary shift, establishing a thriving civilization beneath the deep sea. This underwater society represents a novel species, embracing a unique way of life. Here, all life forms are regarded as equals, fostering a symbiotic relationship that transcends traditional boundaries.

Through centuries of evolution, certain organisms have developed a profound connection with the oceanic humans, acting as external "organs" that coexist and thrive alongside the human body, forming a harmonious symbiosis. This symbiosis is characterized by cellular communion, allowing these organisms to enhance the bodily functions of the oceanic humans, while benefiting from the human lifestyle in return.

The mutualistic relationship extends beyond mere survival, enabling the organisms to aid in their own species' reproduction and continued existence. This era heralds a paradigm shift towards coexistence and mutual prosperity, paving the way for a harmonious future where the oceanic people and marine life coexist in a balanced and equitable manner, emphasizing equality and harmony among species.

Envisioning a possible future utopian world and showing the possible future, thinking about the redefinition of life, and how the world's reincarnation should continue to flourish – as we return to the beginning of all life on Earth, the ocean.

/ 02 Concept

Embedded within this vision is "Organisms Utopia," a speculative design endeavor exploring a groundbreaking paradigm of symbiosis between humanity and Earth's biosphere underwater. It envisions a harmonious symbiotic relationship between humans and the diverse array of life forms on our planet. This project delves into the potential of a utopian future where life flourishes within balanced ecosystems, illustrating how symbiosis between humans and nature can redefine our relationship with the Earth, fostering a harmonious coexistence that benefits all inhabitants of our planet.

In this new utopian world, certain organisms have gradually emerged that can establish a symbiotic relationship with the new oceanic humans. They grow in different habitats in the ocean, each with its own unique characteristics. These organisms, while endowing the new oceanic humans with different abilities, also fulfill some of their needs from oceanic human.

"Organisms Utopia" employs wearable art as a medium, utilizing cutting-edge technologies such as 3D printing, heat-shrinkable sheets, and UV resin to handcraft experimental components for wearable art pieces. These art pieces are meticulously designed to be worn on the head, shoulders, arms, and hands, equipped with various sensors to simulate the sensory experiences of future underwater organisms parasitizing humans. The project aims to convey visual and emotional experiences to its audience, sparking profound reflections and discussions on the relationship between natural organisms and human existence.

/ 04 Research and Analysis

To validate the design opportunity, I conducted surveys and interviews with potential users across industrial safety and emergency response contexts. The findings highlighted several critical pain points in traditional gas mask use:
• Consumable monitoring (72%) – Users cannot accurately detect filter cartridge usage, leading to uncertainty about protection reliability.
• Environmental awareness (86%) – Existing masks provide no real-time feedback on external gas conditions, leaving wearers reactive rather than proactive.
• Communication and ergonomics (80%) – Heavy, bulky equipment reduces comfort and makes verbal communication difficult during high-stress operations.

These insights confirmed that next-generation masks must function as adaptive systems—not only filtering toxins but also sensing, communicating, and supporting decision-making in real time.

/ 06 Outcome and Impact

Chem Guard redefines safety in hazardous environments by merging physical protection with digital intelligence. Workers are no longer shielded in isolation—the mask becomes an active interface, constantly detecting air quality, oxygen supply, and filter status while monitoring the surrounding atmosphere.

At the same time, the mask extends beyond protection by enabling real-time communication with other devices, allowing teams to coordinate instantly, share updates, and respond without delay. Every moment in the field is also captured through live recordings, turning the mask into both a safeguard and an archive of on-site activity.

With live geo-location tracking integrated into the system, each unit is visible and accountable, ensuring rapid support in emergencies. Together, these elements transform Chem Guard into more than equipment—it becomes a connected ecosystem that safeguards individuals while strengthening the collective resilience of the entire operation.

01 Status and Usage Detection

The content shows Chem Guard’s connectivity, airtightness, and oxygen supply status through indicator lights (green for optimal, red for issues), along with filter performance and external gas detection.

02 Real-Time Connectivity

Chem Guard enables real-time communication with other mask users, showing staff status, equipment contacts, call details, and features like adding communicators, adjusting volume, and activating the camera.

03 On-Site Recordings

Chem Guard can record on-site footage, and the interface displays live images and videos captured by the mask in chronological order.

04 Live Geo-Location Connectivity

Chem Guard, when connected, displays its current geographic location through a green equipment sign, providing real-time monitoring of its physical whereabouts.

/ 07 Speculative Extension: TOWARD COGNITIVE RESILIENCE

While Chem Guard addresses immediate physical risks, I began to explore how protection might extend into the cognitive layer. Current research in brain–computer interfaces suggests that neural signals can reveal early signs of cognitive overload and stress. Building on this foundation, I envisioned a future version of Chem Guard that not only detects environmental hazards but also senses a user’s cognitive state in real time.

In this speculative extension, lightweight neural sensors embedded in the device could monitor attention and fatigue, offering subtle feedback before errors occur. The interface might adapt dynamically—simplifying instructions, reducing external distractions, or alerting peers—transforming Chem Guard into a system that safeguards both body and mind.

This proposal does not claim technological readiness, but rather frames a direction: how design could bridge safety and cognition in the era of human–machine symbiosis. It is an exploration of responsibility—what it means to create tools that anticipate not only external threats but also the limits of human resilience.

/ 08 Reflection

This project challenged me to balance between immediate usability and long-term speculation. Through designing Chem Guard, I realized that protective technology cannot be reduced to a single layer of function—it must integrate physical safety, cognitive awareness, and social responsibility. The process also taught me the importance of working across disciplines: combining design, human factors, and emerging technologies shaped not only the product but also my way of thinking.

At the same time, I became aware of the limitations. While the current system provides tangible safeguards, it remains constrained by today’s sensing and processing capabilities. Speculative extensions into cognitive resilience highlight a direction, but require more robust validation from neuroscience and engineering.

Ultimately, the most valuable lesson was that design is not just about solutions, but about framing questions that extend into the future. This reflection has deepened my interest in exploring how brain–computer interfaces and adaptive systems can expand the scope of protection—from the body to the mind—aligning with my pursuit of design at the intersection of technology and human experience.
